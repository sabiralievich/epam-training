Heroes 3

Description

  1. Two players ('1' and '2') have one random creature ('Troglodyte' or 'Minotaur').
  2. Battlefield consists of 26 Cells which have X-coordinate from 0 to 25.
  3. Creatures have method 'move()' which makes creatures to make moves on the battlefield depending on their 'speed' field.
  4. Creatures have 'attack()' method which let them fight each other causing damage depending on their 'damage' field. Value of the damage is calculating for every attack selecting a random value from a range described in 'damage' field and a bonus points calculated in 'superSkillActivate()' method.
  5. Creatures have 'takeDamage()' method which is required for evaluating caused hurt depending on: damage points received from the enemy, creature's health points, creature's damage points and a bonus points received from 'superSkillActivate()' method.
  6. Creatures have 'superSkillActivate()' method which serves for implementing crearure's 'SpecialSkill' field. E.g. Troglodyte has increased attack points, Minotaur has increased defence points. Bonus points are calculating randomly from 0 to the value of 'SpecialSkill' field.
  7. Creatures have 'die()' methods which is called when creature's health is zero or less. Calling this method means that creature is dead and cannot act anymore. As we have only two creatures in this iteration, the game is finishing at this point.
  8. Creatures are deserialized from JSON files (CreatureName.json) by 'gson' library.
  9. Application runs as Thread of 'OneDimensionGame' by calling it's 'run()' method from the 'main()' of Application.java. This was made looking forward to possible Two-dimensional-game in further iterations (Who knows what Gleb will tell us next time...).
